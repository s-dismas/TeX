\Summary{Baldad
\MNote{The eight conflict.}
endeuoreth againe to terrifie Iob, with Gods iudgement, from appealing
therto, and from auouching his owne innocencie.}

But Baldad the Suhite anſwering, ſayd: \V Power and terrour is with him,
that maketh concord in his high ones. \V Is there anie number of his
ſouldiars? and vpon whom shal not his light ariſe? \V
\SNote{Iob anſwered before
\XRef{ch.~9.}
yet blind and obſtinate diſputers ſtil repete the ſame obiections.}
Can man be iuſtified compared with God, or the borne of a woman appeare
cleane? \V Behold the moone alſo doth not ſhine, and the ſtarres are not
cleane in his ſight. \V How much more man rottennes, & the
\Fix{ſome}{ſonne}{obvious typo, fixed in other}
